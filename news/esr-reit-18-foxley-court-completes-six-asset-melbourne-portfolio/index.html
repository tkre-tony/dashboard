A$52.5 Million 18 Foxley Court Acquisition Completes ESR-REIT's Six-Asset Melbourne Freehold Logistics Portfolio — +5.1% Pro Forma DPU Accretion, Leverage to 42.5% Before a Potential S$200 Million Perpetual Issuance

The off-market bolt-on lands sixteen days after the five-asset Melbourne announcement, taking the Proposed Portfolio Acquisition to A$341.1 million across 145,640 sq m of freehold logistics. 18 Foxley Court itself is 100% leased to government-backed…

By PropertyAtlas·23 July 2026·4 min read
Photo: 18 Foxley Court, Derrimut, Melbourne — fully leased to HealthShare Victoria. Credit: ESR-REIT.

ESR-REIT has agreed to acquire a 100% interest in 18 Foxley Court, Derrimut — an institutional-grade freehold logistics property in Melbourne's western industrial precinct — for A$52.5 million (approximately S$47.0 million), completing a six-asset Melbourne portfolio worth A$341.1 million in purchase consideration. The bolt-on, announced on 23 July 2026, joins the five freehold logistics assets unveiled on 7 July and was sourced off-market through the Sponsor's local platform, from Centuria Property Funds No. 4 Limited as trustee for Centuria Industrial Income Trust No. 4.

A government tenant and a 9.5% discount to replacement cost

The property carries 23,229 sq m of gross lettable area on freehold land, is roughly nine years old, and is 100% leased to HealthShare Victoria — a government-backed statutory authority established under Victoria's Health Services Act 1988 — with a weighted average lease expiry of 4.8 years. Jones Lang LaSalle's independent valuation of 17 July 2026 lands at exactly the A$52.5 million purchase price, so the deal is struck at valuation, and at a 9.5% discount to JLL's estimated replacement cost of A$58.0 million. First-year net property income of A$3.0 million puts the entry yield at 5.7% — a notch above the 5.6% blended yield on the full portfolio.

The six-asset portfolio: 145,640 sq m, 12–17% under-rented

With 18 Foxley Court in, the Proposed Portfolio Acquisition comprises six freehold logistics assets across Truganina, Derrimut and Keysborough totalling 145,640 sq m of GLA, with an average building age of about ten years and a tenant roster that includes CEVA Logistics, Silk Logistics, Nick Scali and HealthShare Victoria. Net purchase consideration of A$329.3 million (approximately S$294.9 million) sits at a 1.6% discount to the A$334.7 million market valuation. Occupancy is 92.3% — 100% counting the vendor's one-year rental guarantee of roughly A$2.5 million over the 54% of 39 Naxos Way, Keysborough that is currently vacant — against a portfolio WALE of 3.5 years.

The rental story is the organic-growth case: current average in-place net rents run 12% to 17% below market on Colliers' Q1 2026 Australian industrial data, giving the shorter leases near-term reversion capture, while the longer leases carry contractual escalations either linked to Australian inflation or set between 3.0% and 3.5%.

Funded by recycling — and leverage takes the strain first

The estimated total outlay for the six assets is A$360.1 million (approximately S$322.4 million), funded from the proceeds of ESR-REIT's S$439.1 million divestment programme — the eight non-core Singapore industrial properties and the hotel strata lot at 2 Changi Business Park Avenue 1 — plus debt financing, entirely in SGD-denominated capital at a weighted average all-in cost of 2.49%. No new units will be issued. On a FY2025 pro forma basis the portfolio delivers +5.1% DPU accretion, lifting the distribution from 20.091 to 21.109 Singapore cents, while aggregate leverage steps up from 38.5% to 42.5% and NAV per unit eases 1.0% to S$2.52.

The pressure valve is disclosed alongside: the Manager may issue up to S$200.0 million of subordinated perpetual securities to partially repay the acquisition debt, which would bring leverage back to approximately 38.7% with DPU accretion moderating to +1.6%. Completion is estimated in 3Q 2026, following Foreign Investment Review Board approval in Australia. On its own, 18 Foxley Court is +0.8% DPU accretive and would carry leverage to 39.2%.

What the trade does to the portfolio

The exchange is deliberate: out went non-core Singapore assets with a weighted average remaining land lease of 22.4 years and dated specifications; in comes modern Melbourne freehold. Post-completion, portfolio weighted average land lease tenure extends from 44.9 to 48.2 years, the freehold share of assets rises from 19.3% to 24.2%, and Australia's contribution to rental income climbs from 12.7% to 16.1% — with Singapore still anchoring more than 75% of the enlarged portfolio. Foreign exchange exposure stays contained at 32.3% of total assets, with 97% of JPY investments and 31% of AUD investments funded in matching currencies.

The read

Seven months separate the December 2025 divestment announcement from the estimated completion of the buy-side — a tight recycling loop by S-REIT standards, and the off-market sourcing of the final asset is the Sponsor platform earning its keep. The trade itself is clean: short-tenure Singapore leasehold out, under-rented Melbourne freehold in at a 5.6–5.7% entry yield with built-in escalations. The cost is carried on the balance sheet — gearing at 42.5% is the top of the comfortable range, and the S$200 million perpetual issuance that would bring it back below 39% surrenders most of the headline accretion to do so. At 15.8% of ESR-REIT's S$1,935.7 million market capitalisation, the transaction is discloseable but needs no unitholder vote; the units closed at S$2.395 on 22 July, the day before the announcement.
