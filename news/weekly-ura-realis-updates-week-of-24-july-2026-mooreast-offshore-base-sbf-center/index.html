Mooreast Offshore Base Leads at S$29.7M as GATE+ Adds Eleven — S$100.3M Across 51 C&I Caveats

URA’s 24 July caveat release logs 51 commercial and industrial transactions worth S$100.3 million, led by the S$29.7 million sale of the Mooreast offshore marine base at Shipyard Road in Tuas. A high-floor SBF Center office changed hands at $3,359 psf,…

By PropertyAtlas·24 July 2026·4 min read
Photo: SBF Center, 160 Robinson Road, Singapore. Credit: TK Real Estate.

The week’s largest caveat is a S$29.7 million offshore marine base at Shipyard Road in Tuas, anchoring a 51-caveat batch worth S$100.3 million in this week’s URA REALIS updates. Industrial deals carried S$95.7 million over 48 caveats, while the commercial segment added 3 caveats worth S$4.5 million — among them a high-floor SBF Center office at S$3,359 psf. GATE+ at Tukang Innovation Drive added eleven new-sale caveats, lifting the project to 86 caveats worth S$99.3 million since launch. Of the 51 caveats, 26 have a prior matching transaction in the PropertyAtlas database: 21 resold at a gain and 5 at a loss.

The 51 caveats span 13 postal districts, with Boon Lay leading on 17 (eleven of them GATE+ new sales), Geylang on 6 and Clementi on 4. The master transactions database now stands at 82,779 records.

The anchor — a S$29.7 million offshore marine base in Tuas

The week’s largest trade is the S$29.7 million sale of Mooreast Offshore Base at Shipyard Road — 369,048 sq ft of waterfront land on a 30-year lease from July 2013, translating to S$80 psf on land area with roughly 17 years of tenure remaining. Two further single-user factory deals round out the land action: a 50,388 sq ft facility at Fourth Chin Bee Road at S$7.8 million (S$155 psf on land, 30-year lease from 2007) and a 10,075 sq ft plot at Tuas TechPark at S$5.35 million (S$531 psf on land, 60-year lease from 1993). Together the three land trades account for S$42.9 million — 43% of the week’s value in just three caveats.

GATE+ — take-up momentum, 86 caveats and counting

Eleven new-sale caveats at GATE+ (9 Tukang Innovation Drive) hit the register this week at S$12.1 million, following the 75 lodged in the 17 July release. That lifts the 265-unit, 33-year-leasehold ramp-up B2 development — by SLB Development, Boustead Projects, KSH Holdings and Ho Lee Group — to a cumulative 86 caveats worth S$99.3 million. EdgeProp reported 137 units (52% of the project) sold over the 22–24 May launch weekend; at 86 caveats the register has now captured roughly 63% of those reported sales, up from ~55% last week — the yet-to-lodge balance continuing to surface exactly as expected. Pricing has held the S$490–740 psf band across both batches, with ground-floor units at the top of the range.

Long-hold gains — a 29-year warehouse pays out S$2.35 million

The week’s largest realised gain sits at Tagore Building, where the seller of a ground-floor warehouse unit bought at S$1.64 million in June 1997 and achieved S$3.99 million — a S$2.35 million gain (+143%) over a holding period of 29.1 years, annualised at 3.1%. Far East Plaza continues its run of long-hold payouts: the week’s sole retail caveat traces to a S$690,000 purchase in September 2005, exiting at S$2.2 million — up 219% over 20.8 years, annualised at 5.7%. The percentage champion is Bylands Building, where a S$100,000 office unit bought in October 2006 achieved S$440,000 — a 340% gain over 19.7 years, annualised at 7.8%.

Shorter holds delivered the strongest annualised prints: a Midview City unit bought in June 2023 at S$672,000 achieved S$900,000 in 3.1 years (9.9% annualised), while the Tuas TechPark land parcel returned S$1.35 million on a 2022 purchase of S$4.0 million — 7.6% annualised over four years.

The flat and the falling — five losses, and a 13-year hold that went nowhere

Not every exit paid. The seller of the SBF Center unit fronting this week’s story bought at S$1,856,097 in April 2013 and achieved S$1.88 million — a S$23,903 gain over 13.2 years, annualised at effectively zero. The five outright losses are led by Pioneer Junction, bought at S$485,000 in December 2014 and exiting at S$388,000 — a S$97,000 loss (−20%) over 11.6 years. E-Centre @ Redhill tells both sides of the market in one building: one unit exited at +125% after a 22.9-year hold while a same-floor-plate neighbour gave up 8.7% over 13.5 years. The remaining losses are shallow — Wave9 down 2.1% over a decade, West Connect Building down S$8,000 on an 18-month flip, and an 8 @ Tradehub 21 unit that closed a 9.3-year hold S$320 below cost.

Commercial — three caveats, two of them long-hold exits

The thin commercial column still carried the week’s two highest unit rates: the 560 sq ft SBF Center unit on the 26th floor at S$3,359 psf and the 377 sq ft freehold Far East Plaza unit at S$5,840 psf. The third, a 258 sq ft Bylands Building office at S$440,000 (S$1,703 psf), is a reminder that sub-S$500K office strata entry points still exist on the city fringe.
