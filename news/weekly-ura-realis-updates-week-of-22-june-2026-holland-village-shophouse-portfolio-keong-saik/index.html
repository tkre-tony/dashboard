S$70M Holland Village Shophouse Portfolio Leads the Week — Conservation Deals Total S$94.6M Across Four Properties

Four conservation shophouse deals worth a combined S$94.6 million dominated this week’s 51 new commercial and industrial caveats, led by a S$70 million freehold portfolio at Lorong Liput in Holland Village — the single largest shophouse transaction lodged…

By PropertyAtlas·26 June 2026·6 min read
Photo: The Tong Ah Building (1939) at the junction of Keong Saik Road and Teck Lim Road — the Art Deco landmark at the heart of the Bukit Pasoh Conservation Area. Credit: TKRE.

Four conservation shophouse deals worth a combined S$94.6 million dominated this week’s 51 new commercial and industrial caveats, led by a S$70 million freehold portfolio at Lorong Liput in Holland Village — the single largest shophouse transaction lodged in 2026. Industrial factories made up 35 of the 51 deals by count, but the four shophouses alone accounted for 64% of total transacted value. The batch spans sale dates from 6 April to 19 June 2026, consistent with the usual caveat-lodgement lag.

Heritage on the block — four shophouses, S$94.6 million

The week’s marquee deal was a S$70 million portfolio of three adjoining freehold two-storey shophouses at Lorong Liput in Holland Village (District 10), spanning approximately 7,542 sq ft of land. The properties — at Lorong Liput — were marketed via an expression-of-interest exercise with a guide price of S$60 million. The caveat, lodged on 17 June at S$70 million (S$9,281 psf on land area), represents a 17% premium above the asking price. No prior caveat for these addresses exists in the 31-year REALIS record, making this their first appearance in the public transaction database. Lorong Liput sits at the heart of a dining and lifestyle precinct that has seen continued institutional interest, including the S$100 million sale of Holland Piazza earlier this year.

In the CBD, a freehold conservation shophouse on Keong Saik Road in the Bukit Pasoh Conservation Area changed hands at S$16 million (S$12,715 psf on land area of 1,258 sq ft). The three-storey property — which has a built-up area of approximately 3,305 sq ft — was marketed via separate expression-of-interest exercises, most recently with a guide price of S$17.5 million. The caveat price of S$16 million came in below the guide. However, the P&L tells a different story: the property was last transacted in June 1995 at S$2.1 million, yielding a gain of S$13.9 million — a 662% return over a 31-year hold. Keong Saik Road has undergone a generational transformation from its historical roots into one of Singapore’s most recognisable heritage F&B and hospitality enclaves.

A pair of shophouses on Geylang Road in the Geylang Conservation Area traded at S$6.4 million (S$4,271 psf on land, freehold), also with no prior caveat in the database. At the other end of the spectrum, a 99-year leasehold shophouse on Owen Road sold for S$2.2 million (S$652 psf on land) — a S$300,000 gain after just 2.7 years, having been purchased in October 2023 at S$1.9 million.

The P&L read — 31 gains, 7 losses

Of the 51 caveats, 38 could be paired with a prior transaction at the same address. Of those, 31 were profitable and 7 recorded losses. The deepest loss was a retail unit at EON Shenton, which sold for S$650,000 — down S$308,000 or 32% from its October 2012 purchase at S$958,000 after nearly 14 years of holding. A pair of ground-floor units at iSpace each sold for S$690,000, recording losses of 7–8% against their 2013 purchase prices after more than 13 years. Pioneer Point lost S$130,000 or 15% over a 14-year hold — a pattern among older Jurong West industrial units with shorter remaining lease tenures.

On the gain side, beyond the Keong Saik headline, a single-user factory at Tuas View Loop returned S$1.95 million (186%) over 27 years. Two adjacent ground-floor units at Frontier each gained approximately S$600,000 (60%) over six years — a solid mid-cycle industrial hold. The industrial median PSF of S$482 was in line with recent quarters.

Office and retail — Golden Mile leads the strata segment

The largest office deal was a new-sale unit at The Golden Mile at S$5.5 million (S$3,474 psf), reflecting the repriced positioning of the redeveloped landmark. A ground-floor unit at Orchard Towers traded at S$2.95 million (S$4,645 psf) — a 51% gain on its 2000 purchase after 26 years. A retail unit at Concorde Shopping Centre sold for S$398,000 at S$1,479 psf, while an office unit in the same building changed hands at S$671,600 (S$2,151 psf) — a 163% return on its 1995 purchase.
